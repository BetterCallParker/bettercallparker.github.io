Your Path to Homeownership

I break down the complicated process into simple, manageable steps. Tap the share icon to send any step to your clients.

Step 1

Initial Consultation

I'll discuss your needs, preferences, and budget to create your personalized home search plan. This free consultation helps me understand exactly what you're looking for.

During this meeting, we'll also review your financial readiness, potential timeline, and the current Tucson market conditions to set realistic expectations.

Step 2

Buyer Representation Agreement

Per the new NAR settlement rules, we'll need to sign a Buyer Representation Agreement before viewing homes. This document:

  • Outlines my responsibilities as your agent
  • Details how I'll be compensated (with my $1 promise)
  • Explains what exclusive representation means for you
  • Sets the terms of our professional relationship

I'll walk you through each section and answer all your questions before signing.

Step 3

Home Search & Viewings

I'll help you find properties that match your criteria and schedule convenient viewings. You'll receive my honest feedback about each property's pros and cons.

I'll provide you with:

  • Custom property alerts based on your preferences
  • Detailed Tucson neighborhood information and school ratings
  • Comparable sales data to understand fair market value
  • Identification of potential issues or hidden costs
Step 4

Offer & Earnest Money

When you find the right home, I'll prepare and submit a competitive offer. This includes:

  • Earnest Money Deposit: This "good faith" deposit (typically 1-2% of purchase price) shows sellers you're serious
  • The earnest money is submitted with your offer or within 1-3 business days after acceptance
  • The escrow company will contact you directly to arrange payment, and I'll help coordinate this process
  • This money becomes part of your down payment if the purchase completes successfully
  • I'll include contingencies to protect this deposit if issues arise

I'll negotiate strongly on your behalf for the best price, terms, and to have the seller cover my commission.

Step 5

Due Diligence Period

After your offer is accepted, we enter the 10-day inspection period:

  • I'll help you schedule a comprehensive home inspection
  • We'll review the inspection report together in detail
  • If issues are found, I'll draft a repair request listing items you want the seller to address
  • The seller typically has 3-5 days to respond with one of these options:
    • Accept all repair requests
    • Decline all repair requests
    • Agree to some repairs but not others
    • Offer a credit instead of completing repairs
  • If you're satisfied with their response, we proceed with the purchase
  • If not, you can walk away and receive your earnest money back as long as we're still within the due diligence period

I'll also schedule any additional specialized inspections if needed (termite, radon, etc.).

Step 6

Loan Processing & Appraisal

While inspections are happening, your mortgage lender will be processing your loan:

  • The lender will order an appraisal to verify the home's value
  • You'll need to provide additional financial documentation as requested
  • I'll address any appraisal issues if the home doesn't appraise for the offer price
  • I'll monitor deadlines and keep you updated throughout this process
Step 7

Final Walkthrough

Shortly before closing (usually 24-48 hours), I'll conduct a final walkthrough of the property with you to:

  • Verify all agreed-upon repairs have been completed
  • Confirm the property's condition hasn't changed since your last viewing
  • Ensure all appliances and systems are functioning properly
  • Check that the sellers have removed all their belongings (unless otherwise agreed)
Step 8

Closing Day

The big day has arrived! Here's what to expect:

  • You'll sign numerous documents at the title company or attorney's office
  • I'll attend with you to answer any last-minute questions
  • You'll need to bring a valid photo ID and certified funds for closing costs
  • Once all paperwork is complete and funds have been transferred, you'll receive the keys to your new home!

My support doesn't end at closing - I'm here to help with any questions even after you've moved in.

New NAR Rules Explained Simply

Why Things Are Changing

Following the recent National Association of Realtors (NAR) lawsuit settlement, there are new requirements for buyer broker agreements. These changes are designed to increase transparency in real estate transactions.

What This Means For You:

Written Agreements

You'll now sign a formal agreement before I show you homes. This protects your interests and clearly outlines my responsibilities.

Fee Transparency

All potential costs will be disclosed upfront. You'll know exactly what to expect with no hidden surprises.

Negotiable Fees

Commission rates are fully negotiable, giving you more control over what you pay for services.
